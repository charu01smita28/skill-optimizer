ACME CORPORATION INFORMATION SECURITY POLICY
Version 4.2 — effective 2025-Q3

PART I — SCOPE AND PURPOSE

This policy establishes the information security requirements applicable to all
ACME Corporation employees, contractors, and third-party processors who handle
ACME data or operate ACME systems. The policy aligns with the company's overall
risk management framework and supports our regulatory obligations under
applicable data protection law.

The policy is reviewed annually by the Chief Information Security Officer
(CISO) and material updates are approved by the Risk Committee.

PART II — DATA CLASSIFICATION

All ACME data is classified into one of four sensitivity tiers, applied at the
point of data creation or ingestion:

  - Public: data approved for external publication (e.g., marketing material).
  - Internal: data intended for ACME personnel only; non-disclosure to third parties.
  - Confidential: data with material business or competitive impact if disclosed.
  - Restricted: data subject to regulatory or contractual confidentiality
    obligations, including personal data covered by data protection law.

Each data store, application, and integration must be tagged with the highest
classification of data it processes. Re-classification requires CISO approval.

PART III — ACCESS CONTROL

Access to ACME systems follows role-based access control (RBAC) and the
principle of least privilege. Each role's access scope is documented in the
Role Catalog maintained by IT Security.

Privileged access (administrative or root-level) is granted only via the
Privileged Access Management (PAM) system. PAM sessions are time-bounded and
recorded for audit. All privileged accounts are subject to multi-factor
authentication.

Access reviews are conducted quarterly for production systems and semi-annually
for non-production systems. Stale or excessive access flagged during review
must be revoked or justified within 14 calendar days.

PART IV — ENCRYPTION

Data classified as Confidential or Restricted must be encrypted at rest using
AES-256 (or stronger) and in transit using TLS 1.2 (or higher). Encryption keys
are managed by the central Key Management Service (KMS) operated by the
Platform Security team.

Customer-held encryption keys (BYOK) are supported for Restricted-tier
processing where required by contract; key rotation cadence follows the
contractual obligation or 12 months, whichever is shorter.

Backup data inherits the encryption posture of the source data store. Decrypted
backup material may not be retained outside controlled restoration windows.

PART V — INCIDENT RESPONSE

Suspected security incidents must be reported to the Security Operations
Center (SOC) within 1 hour of detection. The SOC triages and assigns a
severity level (S1 through S4); S1 and S2 incidents trigger the Incident
Response Team (IRT) escalation tree, which includes notification to the CISO
and General Counsel.

External notification timelines follow regulatory requirements: data subjects
and supervisory authorities are notified within 72 hours of confirmed personal
data breach involving Restricted-tier data, or sooner if contractually
required.

Every S1 / S2 incident receives a post-incident review (PIR) within 10 business
days. PIR outputs feed the next quarter's policy and control updates.

PART VI — AUDIT LOGGING

All production systems generate audit logs covering authentication,
authorization, data access, configuration changes, and privileged actions.
Logs are forwarded to the central SIEM in near-real-time.

Log retention is set to 365 days for general audit events and 7 years for
events covering Restricted-tier data access. Log integrity is protected via
write-once storage and cryptographic hashing of log batches.

The Internal Audit team has read-only access to the SIEM for compliance
monitoring; access is logged and reviewed by the CISO.

PART VII — DATA RETENTION AND DISPOSAL

Each data class has a default retention period defined in the Data Retention
Schedule:

  - Public: indefinite, subject to business need.
  - Internal: 5 years from last business use.
  - Confidential: 7 years, or contractual obligation, whichever is longer.
  - Restricted: per regulatory schedule; minimum 6 years.

Data approaching end-of-retention is queued for secure disposal. Disposal of
Restricted-tier data must be witnessed and certified; a Certificate of
Destruction is retained for 10 years.

PART VIII — THIRD-PARTY RISK MANAGEMENT

Vendors and sub-processors handling ACME data undergo a tiered risk assessment
before contracting and annually thereafter. The assessment covers security
posture, financial stability, regulatory standing, and prior incident history.

High-risk vendors (those processing Restricted-tier data or providing critical
infrastructure) are subject to enhanced due diligence including SOC 2 Type II
review, on-site assessment where feasible, and contractual right-to-audit.

Sub-processor changes by approved vendors must be notified to ACME at least 30
days in advance; ACME reserves the right to object on documented security
grounds.

END OF POLICY
