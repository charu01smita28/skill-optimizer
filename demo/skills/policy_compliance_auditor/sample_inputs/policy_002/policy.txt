BRIGHTLEAF LTD INFORMATION SECURITY POLICY
Version 1.0 — effective 2025-08-01

SECTION A — INTRODUCTION

This policy outlines basic information security expectations for all
Brightleaf staff and contractors. The policy is owned by the IT Manager and
reviewed when significant changes are made to the company's systems or
business operations.

The policy applies to all systems owned or operated by Brightleaf and any
data those systems process.

SECTION B — DATA HANDLING

Brightleaf handles two broad categories of data: business data (internal
operational records, customer correspondence, project deliverables) and
personal data (employee records, customer contact details). Both should be
handled with care.

Staff are expected to use good judgment when sharing data. Data shared
externally should be done so via approved channels (work email, the company
file-share). Personal data should not be sent via personal email or messaging
applications.

The IT team maintains a backup of all production systems on a weekly schedule.
Backups are stored on an off-site cloud bucket.

SECTION C — ACCESS AND PASSWORDS

Each user is provisioned an account at hire and the account is disabled at
termination by the IT team. Account creation requires approval by the user's
direct manager.

Passwords must be at least 12 characters long and include a mix of uppercase,
lowercase, numbers, and symbols. Passwords are changed every 90 days.

Multi-factor authentication is enabled for the email system and the company
VPN. Other systems are being assessed for MFA rollout.

SECTION D — DEVICE SECURITY

All company-issued laptops have full-disk encryption enabled by default.
Personal devices (BYOD) used for work email require a screen lock and
device-level encryption where the device supports it.

Laptops are managed by an MDM solution which enforces encryption, OS update
schedules, and the corporate firewall configuration.

Lost or stolen devices must be reported to IT immediately so the device can be
remotely wiped if necessary.

SECTION E — NETWORK AND PERIMETER

The corporate network sits behind a managed firewall with default-deny rules
for inbound traffic. Outbound traffic is filtered through a web proxy that
blocks known-malicious domains and high-risk content categories.

Remote workers connect to internal resources via the company VPN; split
tunneling is disabled. Wi-Fi networks in Brightleaf offices are segregated
between corporate (employee-only) and guest (visitor) SSIDs.

SECTION F — STAFF RESPONSIBILITIES

All staff complete an annual security awareness training module covering
phishing recognition, password hygiene, and reporting suspicious activity.
New starters complete the same training within their first 30 days.

Staff observing a security concern should escalate to their manager or
directly to the IT Manager. The IT Manager is responsible for reviewing
reports and determining whether further action is required.

Sharing of login credentials between staff is prohibited under any
circumstances.

SECTION G — VENDOR USAGE

Brightleaf uses cloud-based services from a small number of established
vendors (e.g., productivity suite, CRM, payroll). Vendors are selected based
on functional fit and pricing.

The IT Manager maintains a register of vendors processing personal or
business-sensitive data and reviews the register annually for any changes.

END OF POLICY
